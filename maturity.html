User Guide

Welcome! This tool is designed to help you and your team understand your current strengths and opportunities in applying Lean principles. Think of it as a guided self-assessment.

Your progress is saved automatically in your web browser, so you can leave and come back to it at any time.

Step 1: Fill in Your Assessment Details

Before you begin, you'll see a form at the top of the page called the "User Data Plate."

It's helpful to fill this in, as the information will be used to title your exported reports.

  • Maturity Assessment - [Project/Department Name]: The name of the project or team being assessed.
  • Org: Your company or organization's name.
  • Date of Assessment: The date you are performing the assessment.
  • Assessed By: Your name.
  • Scope: A brief sentence describing what this assessment covers (e.g., "The manufacturing process for Product X").

Step 2: Exploring the Concepts (The Flashcards)

The main screen shows a grid of flashcards. Each card represents a key Lean concept with a guiding question.

  • Colors: The different colors represent different themes, like "Foundation" or "Leadership & Culture."
  • Gold Border: Cards with a gold border are "Gateway" questions. These are critical concepts you generally need to have in place before you can effectively move to the next level of Lean maturity.
  • Action: Simply click on any card to open a detailed view and assess yourself.

Step 3: Assessing a Concept (Inside the Pop-up Window)

After clicking a card, a detailed window will appear. Here you can learn about the concept and rate your team.

  1. Understand the Concept:
    • On the left, you'll see the main question, an image, and Example Guidance to help you understand what success looks like.
    • On the right, you'll find the Objective of the concept and the Scoring Criteria, which explains what each score from 1 to 5 means.
  2. Rate Yourself:
    • At the bottom, use the Self-Assessment slider to rate your team's current maturity from 1 (Absent) to 5 (Embedded). For the first three questions, you will use simple Yes/No radio buttons.
  3. Add Notes:
    • Use the Comments & Actions box to write down your thoughts, evidence, or ideas for improvement. This is very useful for your final report.
  4. Update the Card's Status:
    • Mark as Evaluated: Click this button when you are finished assessing the card. It will get a petrol border on the main screen, and its score and comments will be added to your dashboard.
    • Mark as Mastered: If you are learning the questions and you have understood this concept, you can click this. The card will be faded out on the main screen to show that it's understood.

When you're done with a card, click the 'X' in the top-right corner or press the Esc key to close the window.

Step 4: Viewing Your Results (The Dashboard)

At any time, you can see a summary of your results by clicking the purple View Dashboard button at the top of the page.

Here you will find:

  • Current Lean Maturity Level: An overall summary of your progress on a 5-stage timeline.
  • Gateway Status: A quick check to see if you've passed the critical "Gateway" questions for each level.
  • Progress by Category: A bar chart showing your average score in each theme.
  • Maturity Profile: A "spider web" chart that gives you a great visual of your strengths and weaknesses.
  • Evaluated Questions & Comments: A list of all the questions you marked as "Evaluated," along with the scores and comments you entered.

Managing Your Assessment Data

At the top of the page, there are several buttons to help you manage your work:

  • Export Results (CSV): This saves all your scores and comments into a CSV file, which can be opened by programs like Microsoft Excel or Google Sheets. This is a great way to create a backup of your work.
  • Import Results (CSV): This allows you to load a previously saved assessment from a CSV file. Warning: This will overwrite any progress currently saved in your browser.
  • Export to PDF: (Inside the Dashboard) This creates a professional-looking, multi-page report containing your dashboard visuals and a detailed list of all your answers and comments. It's perfect for sharing with your team or leadership.
  • Reset Progress: This red button will erase all your progress and start the assessment over from the beginning. A confirmation message will appear to prevent you from doing this by accident.
